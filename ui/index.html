LATE CITY FINAL — THE LAUNCH EDITION

Your Terminal Prints
a Newspaper Now

— and it already knows where you left off —

Every developer wakes to the same cold case: what was I doing yesterday? The tabs are gone. The terminal scrolled away. The brilliant plan from 11:48 p.m. lives in a session file nobody reads. paper reads it. It combs your Claude Code and Codex transcripts, your git history, your unread mail and the morning wire — and an AI editorial desk writes you a front page about your own life.

There is no journaling. There is no app. There is one command, and then there is a newspaper: a headline about your work, the threads you left hanging, your calendar above the fold, the news annotated with why it matters to you, and three suggestions for the day — terse, correct, and slightly bossy, the way a good editor should be.

The paper knows when it is being read. At eight it is the morning edition; at one a.m. it is the late night edition, and its lead paragraph writes for that hour. Editions are cached — the first read of the day does the work, and every read after is instant, like a paper that has already landed on the porch.

Sources are connectors: drop a Python file in a folder and your markets desk, training log, or arXiv column appears in tomorrow’s edition. A broken connector never stops the presses. Nothing needs an API key — the writing engine is whichever agent CLI you already use, claude or codex, driven headlessly.

HOW TO SUBSCRIBE

git clone github.com/sayantan94/newspaper
cd newspaper
uv tool install --editable .
paper

Delivery begins immediately. The first edition backfills a fortnight of your history; thereafter the paper is waiting before the coffee is.
